There once was a mighty king, who reigned upon the {wetland_nation}. Under his ruling the people suffered, there was not enough food, no place to live, and the forced labour for the noble family was unbearable. Everyone was scared of him. It's told the king once wanted to start a war with the northern people and ordered his people to go and cut down the sacred mangrove forest, to make materials for the needed navy.

One of the woodcutters was called {woodcutter} and when he heard the orders to disrespect the departed souls, he was filled with rage and ran to the king, "You have no rights to that sanctuary" he exclaimed, the king leaned forward and said "You think you little peasant can tell me what I can and cannot do?!", he chuckled "Well I will have to teach you how to act toward your king." and then he ordered "Take him to the dungeon, he will be executed the next day!". The guards dragged poor {woodcutter} out of the king's sight into the holding cells, where prisoners wait for their execution.

{woodcutter} was at his lowest, he couldn't believe what just happened, he will be executed the next day and the mangrove forest will be destroyed and he can't do anything about it. The next day {woodcutter} was taken to the execution site, but then he remembered "I want my last wish! Before you take everything from me.". The executioner was a good person underneath all the blood so he asked "And what would a person like you wish for the last time?", {woodcutter} responded "I want to pray for the last time.", "Well granted, you may pray for the one last time.", {woodcutter} knelt down and prayed for the gods to take revenge for him, his peers and the sanctuary that was about to get destroyed, he begged the gods to free {wetland_nation} of suffering. Then he looked one last time at the sky and slam! Quiet... it was done.

That night the seas were exceptionally rough. The god of water, {beget_god_of_water} took notice of the latest events and decided to fulfill the prayer. He brought the Great Flood upon {wetland_nation}, the water engulfed the city, broke all the dams, took down bridges. Streams surged through the canals splashing on the docks, huge waves crashing against the houses. The nation felt what was happening, everyone knew what fate had befallen {woodcutter} and that night each and everyone of them decided to make it all right and bring equality to the {wetland_nation}, they united and stormed toward the king's house, even the guards turned against him. Everything collapsed in moments single decision but the king's fate was already set in stone. All the cruel ruling led to this moment, it's told that in the chaos he was brutally murdered, torn apart and thrown into the stormy seas which satisfied {beget_god_of_water} and the storms calmed down.

From that night on, all begets stood equal, and {beget_god_of_water} was forever known not only as the god of water, but also as the god of equality.
